What Is a CPS Test?

A CPS test (clicks per second test) measures how many times you can click a mouse button within a given time period. Our test offers 8 durations: 1, 2, 5, 10, 15, 30, 60, and 100 seconds. The average person clicks at 6–7 CPS in normal clicking. Trained gamers using jitter or butterfly technique can exceed 14 CPS.

Clicking Techniques Explained

Regular Clicking

Standard index finger clicking. Average: 6–8 CPS. Sustainable for extended periods without fatigue or injury risk. Best for casual gaming and everyday computer use.

Jitter Clicking

Tensing forearm and wrist muscles to create rapid vibrations translating into 10–14 CPS. Used in Minecraft PvP. Carries injury risk (repetitive strain) with prolonged use. Not recommended for more than 10-minute sessions.

Butterfly Clicking

Using two fingers alternating on the same mouse button to achieve 15–25 CPS. Banned in many competitive Minecraft servers. High CPS but reduced accuracy for directional clicking tasks.

Drag Clicking

Dragging a finger across the mouse button to generate 30–40+ CPS through friction. Highly hardware-dependent. Causes physical damage to mouse buttons over time. Excluded from most official records.
