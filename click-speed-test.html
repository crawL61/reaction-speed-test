What Is a CPS Test?

A CPS test (clicks per second test) measures how many times you can click a mouse button within a set time. Our test offers 8 durations: 1, 2, 5, 10, 15, 30, 60, and 100 seconds. The average person clicks at 6–7 CPS in normal clicking. Trained gamers using advanced techniques can exceed 14 CPS.

Clicking Techniques Explained

Regular Clicking

Standard index finger clicking. Average: 6–8 CPS. Sustainable for extended periods without fatigue or injury risk. Best for casual gaming and everyday computer use.

Jitter Clicking

Tensing forearm muscles to create rapid vibrations: 10–14 CPS. Used in Minecraft PvP. Carries repetitive strain injury risk with prolonged use. Not recommended for sessions longer than 10 minutes.

Butterfly Clicking

Alternating two fingers on the same button: 15–25 CPS. Banned on many competitive Minecraft servers. High CPS but reduced directional accuracy.

Drag Clicking

Dragging a finger across the button to generate 30–40+ CPS through friction. Highly hardware-dependent and causes physical damage to mouse buttons over time.

How to Improve Your CPS Score

  • Warm up: Shake out your hand and do light finger stretches before testing.
  • Correct posture: Keep your elbow on a flat surface. Floating your arm causes faster fatigue.
  • Mouse weight: Lighter mice (under 80g) allow faster clicking with less effort.
  • Click depth: Use a mouse with a short actuation distance — less travel means faster cycling.
  • Daily practice: 5–10 minutes of consistent practice increases CPS by 1–2 within a week.
  • Test duration: Your 1-second CPS will always be higher than your 10-second CPS due to fatigue. Train longer durations for Minecraft PvP endurance.
