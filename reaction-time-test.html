What Is a Reaction Time Test?

A reaction time test measures the elapsed time between a visual stimulus appearing and your voluntary response — clicking the mouse or tapping the screen. Results are expressed in milliseconds (ms). Our free online test runs 5 rounds and calculates your average, best, and a performance rating compared to global human benchmarks.

The test uses the browser's performance.now() API for sub-millisecond timing accuracy. A random delay of 1.5–4 seconds prevents anticipatory clicking. Clicking before the signal invalidates the round. This methodology matches the standards used in academic research on human reaction time.

Average human reaction time to a visual stimulus is 200–250ms. This includes ~20ms for light to travel from monitor to eye, ~40–50ms for the visual signal to reach the brain's visual cortex, ~50–80ms for the motor cortex to generate a response signal, and ~30–50ms for the nerve signal to travel to your finger muscles.

How to Improve Your Reaction Time

  • Sleep: Even one night of poor sleep (under 6 hours) adds 30–80ms to reaction time. Prioritize 7–9 hours.
  • Daily practice: Training reaction tests for 10–15 minutes daily builds neural efficiency over 2–4 weeks.
  • Action games: Fast-paced FPS games (CS2, Valorant, Apex Legends) measurably improve reaction speed. Studies show gamers react 50–100ms faster than non-gamers on average.
  • Caffeine: 100–200mg reduces reaction time by 10–30ms temporarily. Effects peak 30–60 minutes after consumption.
  • Hydration: Mild dehydration (1–2% body weight) significantly slows neural processing speed.
  • Monitor refresh rate: A 144Hz monitor reduces display lag from ~8ms (60Hz) to ~3ms, directly improving test scores.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good reaction time for gaming?

For competitive gaming, under 200ms is excellent. Most professional FPS players average 160–180ms. However, reaction time is only one component of skill — game sense, crosshair placement, and decision-making matter more at higher levels.

Why does my reaction time vary between rounds?

Natural variation of 20–40ms between rounds is completely normal. Factors include momentary attention fluctuations, slight differences in sitting position, and natural neural variability. Taking the 5-round average smooths out this variance and gives a reliable baseline score.

Does the reaction test work on mobile?

Yes, but mobile scores typically read 30–80ms higher than desktop due to touchscreen latency (~30ms) and 60Hz display refresh cycles. For the most accurate results, use a desktop with a wired mouse and 144Hz+ monitor.

What is the world record reaction time?

The fastest verified human visual reaction time is approximately 100ms, recorded in laboratory conditions. For sprinters, the International Athletics Federation sets 100ms as the minimum legal reaction time in the 100m dash — responses faster than this are ruled false starts.
