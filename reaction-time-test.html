What Is a Reaction Time Test?

A reaction time test measures the elapsed time between a visual stimulus and your voluntary response. Results are in milliseconds (ms). Our test runs 5 rounds and shows your average, best, and a rating vs global benchmarks.

The test uses performance.now() for sub-millisecond accuracy. A random 1.5–4 second delay prevents anticipation. Early clicks invalidate the round.

How to Improve Your Reaction Time

  • Sleep 7–9 hours: One bad night adds 30–80ms.
  • Daily practice: 10–15 min/day builds speed in 2–4 weeks.
  • Play FPS games: CS2/Valorant players react 50–100ms faster than non-gamers.
  • Caffeine: 100–200mg cuts reaction time by 10–30ms.
  • Stay hydrated: Mild dehydration slows neural processing significantly.
  • 144Hz monitor: Cuts display lag from ~8ms to ~3ms.
  • Wired mouse: Removes 1–8ms wireless polling delay.

The Science

Visual reaction involves four phases: photoreceptor activation (~10ms), signal transmission (~40–50ms), cognitive processing (~50–80ms), and motor execution (~30–50ms). Total minimum: ~150ms.

Reaction time peaks at age 18–24 and increases ~2ms/year after. Regular training significantly slows this decline.
