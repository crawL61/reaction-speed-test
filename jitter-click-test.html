What Is Jitter Clicking?

Jitter clicking involves tensing your forearm and wrist muscles to create rapid involuntary vibrations in your finger, translating into 10–14 clicks per second. It is one of the most common techniques used in competitive Minecraft PvP, where higher CPS can provide a combat advantage in certain game versions.

The technique was popularized on YouTube around 2013–2015 and quickly spread through the Minecraft community. Today it is also used by players wanting to maximize CPS scores without butterfly or drag clicking.

How to Jitter Click

  1. Place your index finger on the left mouse button
  2. Rest your arm comfortably on a flat surface
  3. Tense your forearm muscles firmly — not painfully
  4. The vibration from the tension translates to rapid clicks
  5. Practice holding the tension for 5–10 seconds at a time

Important: Jitter clicking carries real injury risk. Repetitive strain injuries (RSI), carpal tunnel symptoms, and wrist pain are common with overuse. Never practice for more than 10 minutes at a time and stop immediately if you feel pain.
