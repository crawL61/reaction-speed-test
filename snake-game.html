How to Play Snake

The classic Snake game is one of the most iconic video games of all time, popularized by Nokia mobile phones in the late 1990s. You control a snake moving continuously across the grid. Eat food (glowing dots) to grow longer and score points. The game ends if the snake hits the wall or its own body.

Our version features a 20×20 grid with smooth controls, support for keyboard (Arrow Keys / WASD) and mobile swipe input, and progressive difficulty that increases speed every 5 food items consumed.

Strategy: Space Management

High scores in Snake are won through space management, not speed. As the snake grows, the available grid shrinks. The most reliable high-score strategy is to follow a systematic path pattern — specifically a modified spiral that visits most grid cells before collecting food, leaving escape routes open at all times.

Beginners should avoid chasing food directly. Instead, maintain a consistent path direction and allow food to come to you. Sudden direction changes to grab food often lead to self-collisions in the mid-game when the snake body occupies 30%+ of the grid.
