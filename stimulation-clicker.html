What Is Aim Training?

Aim training refers to deliberate practice of mouse accuracy and target acquisition speed. Studies on FPS players show that dedicated aim training 15–20 minutes daily produces measurable accuracy improvements within 2–3 weeks. Our stimulation clicker presents randomized moving targets to simulate the unpredictability of real gameplay.

Three Difficulty Modes

  • Easy: Large targets, slow movement. Ideal for beginners building fundamental accuracy habits.
  • Medium: Standard targets and speed. Represents typical FPS game enemy hitbox size.
  • Hard: Small fast targets. Challenges even experienced FPS players. Recommended for CS2 and Valorant competitive players.

How to Improve Aim

  • Mouse sensitivity: Most professional CS2 players use 400–800 DPI with 1.5–2.5 in-game sensitivity. Lower sensitivity allows more precise micro-adjustments.
  • Mousepad size: A large (XL) mousepad allows full arm sweeps for 180-degree turns without lifting the mouse.
  • Crosshair placement: Keeping your crosshair at head height pre-aimed at common angles reduces required flick distance by 60–80%.
  • Consistent grip: Choose one grip style (palm, claw, fingertip) and stick with it. Switching grips mid-practice prevents muscle memory formation.
  • Warm up: 5–10 minutes of aim training before playing measurably improves in-game performance for 30–60 minutes.
