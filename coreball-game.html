How to Play Coreball

Coreball is a precision timing game where a ball orbits a central core. You shoot additional balls by clicking or tapping — your goal is to attach them to the core without hitting any balls already placed there. The core rotates continuously, and the rotation speed increases with each successful shot, making later rounds significantly more challenging.

The game tests both pattern recognition (identifying rotation rhythm) and impulse control (waiting for the right gap rather than clicking reactively). Players who score well on our Reaction Time Test also tend to excel at Coreball due to overlapping skills.

Strategy Guide

Watch ahead, not at current position

By the time your ball reaches the core, the rotation will have advanced. Aim where the gap will be when your ball arrives, not where it is now. This predictive aiming — called "leading the target" — is also fundamental to FPS games.

Find the rhythm first

Spend the first few shots observing the rotation speed before committing. Identifying the rhythm is more valuable than scoring quickly.

Use the gaps between clusters

As more balls attach, clusters form. Large gaps between clusters offer the safest shooting windows. Target the center of gaps rather than edges.
