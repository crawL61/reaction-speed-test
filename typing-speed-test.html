What Is a Typing Speed Test?

A typing speed test measures how many words per minute (WPM) you can type accurately. Our test tracks real-time WPM and accuracy percentage as you type. The global average typing speed is 40 WPM for casual typists; professional typists average 65–75 WPM.

How to Improve Typing Speed

Touch Typing

Touch typing — using all 10 fingers without looking at the keyboard — is the single biggest upgrade. Home row position (ASDF / JKL;) is the foundation. Most people see a 20–40 WPM increase after mastering touch typing.

Consistent Practice

15–20 minutes of focused daily practice produces measurable improvement within 2–3 weeks. Prioritize accuracy over speed — errors slow you down more than careful typing.

Keyboard Choice

Mechanical keyboards with linear switches (Cherry MX Red, Speed Silver) have short actuation distances that support faster typing. However, any keyboard can achieve 80+ WPM with proper technique.

Text Practice

Practice with common word combinations rather than random characters. The 200 most common English words make up 50% of written text — mastering these transitions alone dramatically improves speed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most data entry positions require 45–60 WPM minimum. Administrative assistant roles typically require 60+ WPM. Court reporters and medical transcriptionists often need 225+ WPM using stenography equipment.
WPM is calculated as (characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed. A "word" is standardized as 5 characters including spaces. Errors are subtracted from the final score — one uncorrected error typically costs 1 WPM.
Starting from hunt-and-peck typing, most people reach 60 WPM in 2–4 months of consistent daily practice (15–20 min/day). Learning touch typing first may slow you down temporarily before your speed increases significantly.
