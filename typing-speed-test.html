What Is a Typing Speed Test?

A typing speed test measures how many words you type per minute (WPM) with a given accuracy percentage. Our test presents a passage of common English words and measures your speed and accuracy in real time. No signup required — results are displayed immediately after you finish typing.

WPM is calculated as total characters typed divided by 5 (the standardized word length), divided by elapsed minutes. Accuracy is measured per keystroke. Our test tracks corrections made with backspace separately from uncorrected errors.

The global average typing speed is approximately 40 WPM. Professional typists average 65–75 WPM. The world record is 212 WPM over one minute, set by Barbara Blackburn using the Dvorak keyboard layout.

How to Type Faster

  • Learn touch typing: Using all 10 fingers on home row keys is the single most effective improvement. Initial learning takes 2–4 weeks with daily practice.
  • Practice daily: 15–20 minutes of focused typing practice yields measurable improvement within 2 weeks.
  • Prioritize accuracy first: Speed follows accuracy naturally. Typing fast with errors is slower overall than typing accurately at moderate speed.
  • Use proper posture: Wrists level with keyboard, fingers curved, elbows at 90 degrees. Correct posture prevents fatigue that limits speed.
