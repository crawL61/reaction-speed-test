How Age Affects Reaction Time: Complete Science Guide

Published by Reaction Speed Test · 6 min read

Reaction time follows a predictable biological curve. It improves rapidly through childhood, peaks between 18-24, then gradually declines. Here is what the science says.

Why Does Reaction Time Peak at 18-24?

The peak corresponds to the completion of prefrontal cortex development and maturation of myelin sheaths — the fatty insulation around nerve fibers that speeds signal transmission. Both reach peak efficiency in the early 20s.

Why Does It Decline After 25?

  • Myelin sheaths gradually thin, slowing signal transmission ~1-2ms per year after 30
  • Reduced dopamine and acetylcholine levels
  • Slower visual processing and contrast sensitivity

Can You Slow the Decline?

Yes — significantly. Active older adults maintain reaction times 30-50ms faster than sedentary peers. The most effective interventions:

  • Regular aerobic exercise — 3+ times per week maintains cerebral blood flow
  • Cognitive training — daily reaction tests and video games strengthen neural pathways
  • Quality sleep — 7-9 hours consolidates neural efficiency
  • Low stress — chronic stress accelerates cognitive aging

Experience Compensates for Age

Experienced older players often match younger players in actual game performance despite slower measured reaction times. Pattern recognition, pre-aiming and game sense reduce required reaction time — which is why veterans remain competitive well into their 30s and 40s.
